1.2

To successfully complete a digital project, whether it's a website, game or an app, you must create a plan of the content/storyline etc. Tools like Trello are great for outlining a to-do list which follows a Kanban format, so when you've completed that task, you can easily keep track of what's still a work in progress, what's been done and what is still left to do. Depending on what type of project you're making, you will need to use different coding languages or a mixture of them, (Python for back end and CSS for front end.) You will also require someone on your team that posses those skills, or you will need them yourself if you are working individually. Communicating with your team is extremely important, keeping team mates updated with what you're doing and knowing what's going on their end will help in a few ways, here are three: 1.If anyone's ahead of schedule, they can help out with anyone in need. 2.Sharing new ideas/changes to the project. 3.Feedback on your own work and a different perspective on what you've done. Even if you're working on your own, you need to ensure good communication with the client as you want to make sure you are fulfilling the brief as best as you possibly can. Having a team leader is also helpful, although not essential. It may be more beneficial for teams with less experience to have someone with more to act as a mentor and guide them in the right direction if having trouble.

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) operates in the UK and Europe and from May 2018, they introduced new regulations, designed to inhibit the misuse of personal data."Data" is anything that can identify a person, such as their phone number, email, photos and ip address. GDPR's impact on digital projects has meant that businesses have to take new measures in which to store, organise, protect and process customer data; also having available information to let the customer know how and what will happen to their data after it is harvested. Failure to comply with the rules can result in a fine upwards of a million pounds. This can be a problem for people that have made a website for their business and they are not aware of GDPR's guidelines, perhaps because they were teaching themselves from online resources and no one mentioned them. This can be problematic if someone hacks into a non protected data base and steals and misuses the data. You will still be liable to be charged as you did not set up a safe environment of which to store customer data.

3.2

My first advice to improve productivity and efficiency would be to fully utilise the tools already at your disposal. In this case, Slack and Trello. These act as a map towards your end goal and should be referred to as much as possible. Sometimes that means badgering your tutor or classmates if you're not sure what's going on. It’s better to ask sooner rather than later! When taking on a big project like making a website, you should track how much time you are spending on each tasks and allocate so much time to a task. This paired with regular breaks can keep you from feeling drained and burnt out. The Pomodoro Method is an excellent example and I followed throughout my project; you set a timer for 25 minutes and work until time is up and then you have a 10 minute break to do whatever you want! I find this reduces the urge to look at distractions such as social media, messages because I know if I need to I can respond or scroll in my 10 minute break. I also found myself coming up with new ideas on my breaks so had new cool things to add to the project.

Copyright is a legal term, which means in order to use a product(piece of music, images, research, articles, art) wherever you want, you must purchase the right to use that image for "x" number of years unless you are the creator of that product. An example of copyright infringement is downloading free music from sites that are not directly connected to the artist. The same goes with films, and other forms of digital media. Other examples include seeing someone's art online and using their image to print T-shirts and claiming the design is your own. To avoid being sued, plenty of stock images exist to use instead or get int contact with the owner. If they are famous, you will usually have to cough up a lot of money.




2.6

Technology is seen in a great light in the medical field by enabling the less abled to do what they wouldn't of been able to do before. Look at Stephen Hawking for instance! However, in daily life, when using a laptop or tablet to access education, shopping, social media or other sites that provide a service or host a governmental obligation, this shut out a large number of people. Differences in disability means there is a wide range of incompatibilities that disabled internet users face. People with visual impairments face the challenge of not being able to read off a screen, and navigate their way through a site. While others that may have issues with dexterity may find it difficult to move easily within the page which can cause frustration. Deaf individuals don't always have access to subtitles on platforms such as YouTube and other video media sites. To combat this, when creating websites and other forms of digital interactive platforms, take into account the difficulties that some people may face. Ensuring that deaf people stay connected with subtitles. Blind people have audio guidance throughout sites, and people with challenged motor skills have easier navigation. A lot of these solutions may have to be niche to its own disability as different severities of each disability exist but it is something to think about when creating future digital projects.
